# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Open Studio is used by creators, YouTubers, TikTokers, social media managers, small agencies, and open-source contributors who want to turn a rough content idea into publish-ready creative assets without jumping across several tools. The primary context is a desktop creator workflow: paste a brief, generate or refine scripts, thumbnails, music, assets, and pipelines, then organize exports.

## Product Purpose

Open Studio is an open-source creative workspace for content production. It helps users generate scripts, thumbnail concepts, captions, descriptions, hashtags, music, and pipeline outputs through configurable AI providers. Success means the dashboard feels like a premium creative control room while keeping the core workflow direct: idea in, organized assets out.

## Brand Personality

Premium, cinematic, precise. The product should feel creative and modern without becoming noisy or generic. It needs enough polish to feel trustworthy, but the interface should still serve fast creator decisions instead of turning into a marketing page.

## Anti-references

Do not make the product feel like a generic AI SaaS dashboard, a cold analytics console, or a colorful template marketplace. Avoid oversaturated gradients, busy charts, inflated marketing copy, light-mode-first composition, and decorative UI that distracts from creating content.

## Design Principles

1. Make creation feel immediate: each screen should make the next creative action obvious.
2. Keep the workspace cinematic but quiet: use depth, shadows, dark surfaces, and restrained magenta accents with discipline.
3. Treat assets as the product memory: thumbnails, scripts, music, and exports should feel organized and easy to revisit.
4. Build reusable visual language: the dashboard is the base for future screens, so components must be consistent and maintainable.
5. Preserve creator focus: avoid extra copy, fake metrics, and elements that do not support the current task.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Use semantic landmarks, keyboard focus states, sufficient contrast on dark surfaces, and reduced-motion fallbacks. The desktop dashboard is the priority, but the layout should remain navigable on smaller screens by stacking content rather than clipping it.
